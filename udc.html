What are UDCs (UISCs)?

UDCs are government‑backed, entrepreneur‑run digital service hubs in nearly every union (≈4,500+), offering trusted, affordable access to e‑services. They provide the perfect last‑mile for health services.

  • Coverage Presence in almost every union; deep community trust
  • Infra PCs, internet, printer, webcam; run by trained entrepreneurs
  • Proven model Enabled agent banking, e‑gov, and mobile payments at scale

Bank Asia — Proof at Scale

  • 5,000+ rural agent outlets via UDCs
  • 7.3M agent accounts; 64% women; 91% rural
  • ~Tk 6,000 crore deposits; 3.5M G2P beneficiaries

Strategic Offerings via UDC

  • Prescription Delivery: Order & pickup authentic meds at the UDC
  • Telemedicine: Doctor video consults with UDC facilitation
  • Diagnostics Access: Book tests; sample collection camps
  • Health Products: OTC, hygiene, mother & child care items

Implementation Blueprint & Rollout

Implementation Blueprint

  1. MoU & Alignment: Sign with a2i; onboard UDC & Union leaders.
  2. Agent Portal: Lightweight UDC web/app for orders, consults, tracking.
  3. Training & SOPs: Pharmacovigilance, privacy, triage, device basics.
  4. Infra Check: Webcam, headset, privacy partition; mini cooler for cold‑chain.
  5. Workflows: eRx verification → dispatch → UDC pickup; consult slots; lab camps.
  6. Logistics Hubs: District hubs + milk‑run routes to UDCs (≤48h delivery).
  7. Data & QA: Dashboards, SLAs, audits; agent incentives + coaching.
  8. Community Outreach: Market‑day demos, imam/school info, success stories.

12‑Month Rollout (Phased)

Phase 1 (M1–M3): 50–100 UDCs
Pilot 1–2 districts; refine ops; early marketing.
Phase 2 (M4–M6): 200–400 UDCs
Expand to 4–5 districts; add hubs; steady demand.
Phase 3 (M7–M9): 600–800 UDCs
Accelerate onboarding; optimize supply chain.
Phase 4 (M10–M12): 1,000 UDCs
Consolidate quality; prep all‑union scale‑up.

Total setup & scale costs ≈ BDT 10–12M (platform, training, outreach, initial logistics). Variable delivery costs recovered via fees.

Risks & Mitigations

Key Risks

  • Adoption lag: Rural trust & awareness build‑up takes time.
  • Supply variance: Cold‑chain & stockouts in remote areas.
  • Process drift: Inconsistent SOPs across entrepreneurs.
  • Connectivity: Unreliable internet/power in some unions.

Mitigations

  • Local champions, market‑day demos, testimonial loops.
  • District hubs, insulated packs, reorder thresholds, alternates.
  • Certification, audits, coaching, incentives for SLA adherence.
  • Offline queuing, SMS fallbacks, solar/UPS at UDCs.

12‑Month Outcomes

  • ~148k total customer interactions in Year 1
  • ~116k prescriptions delivered; ~29k tele‑consults
  • ~BDT 118.9M gross revenue; net ≈ 15%
  • Brand presence in 1,000 unions; ready to scale nationwide
